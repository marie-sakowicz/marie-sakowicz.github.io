About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Human-Centered Computing at the University of Maryland Baltimore County (UMBC). My research journey is driven by a deep passion for making technology accessible and beneficial for all learners, particularly focusing on special education and assistive technologies.

With a background in education and technology, I bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical applications. My work primarily focuses on developing and implementing AI-enabled technologies that enhance educational experiences for students with diverse learning needs.

Research Interests

  • Special Education Technology
  • Assistive Technology
  • Universal Design for Learning
  • Human-Computer Interaction
  • Accessible Computing

Current Research

My current research explores the intersection of artificial intelligence and special education, with a particular focus on developing AI-enabled tools that support teachers in creating personalized learning experiences for students with autism spectrum disorder (ASD). This work involves close collaboration with educators, students, and families to ensure that the technology solutions developed are both effective and user-friendly.
